Maria Callas

The famous Greek Opera Singer

Here's a time line of Maria Callas' life:

  • 1923 December 2, Born Sophie Cecilia Kalos; in New York City, U.S., from a Greek immigrant family.
  • 1937 Maria moves back to Greece with her mother.
  • 1940 Originally her auditions are not going well until she is asked to audition for the General Manager of the Metropolitan Opera. He immediately offers her the leading roles in two productions of the 1946/7 season.
  • 1940 Later on in the year she mahes a professional stage debut at the age of 17 in Boccaccio. 1942 She is asked to perform in Tosca at the Athens Opera.
  • 1949 January, I Puritani is performed in Venice shortly after starring the Italian soprano Margherita Carosio as Elvira. One night, Maria begins sight-reading Elvira's music. Serafin's wife hears Maria. She is offered the role of Elvira as Carosio has fallen ill.
  • 1949 April, She marries Giovanni Battista Meneghini, a man almost 30 years older than Maria.
  • 1950 December, La Scala, the famous Milan theatre, finally surrenders to Maria Callas. She opens the 1950/1 season with I Vespri Siciliani and is greeted with thunderous applause and enthusiastic reviews.
  • 1952 July, Maria signs an exclusive recording contract with Walter Legge, director of EMI.
  • 1955 November, The day that establishes Maria's image as a tigress. She has just finished performing Madama Butterfly, when she is summoned to court - she is being sued by a former manager.
  • 1956 October, Maria finally makes her debut at the Metropolitan Opera as Norma in Bellini's Norma.
  • 1958 January, She is scheduled to sing in a gala performance of Norma at the Rome Opera House. The performance is to be attended by Italy's president, Giovanni Gronchi and his wife. Unfortunately, Maria has been seen drinking champagne and staying up very late.Next day she was not able to sing at the performace she was scheduled. It was a disastrous day in her career!
  • 1959 September, Maria announces that she will be parting from her husband and begins a love affair with Aristotle Onassis. He leaves her for Jackie Kennedy, JFK's widow.
  • 1969 June, Maria begins work on a film of Medea with Pier Paolo Pasolini. She hardly sings but still works very hard. So hard that one day she faints after running on a dry riverbed in the sun for a particular shot. Unfortunately for Maria, the film is not a success.
  • 1970 May, Maria's singing career comes to a quick halt. She is rushed to the hospital and it is announced that she had tried to commit suicide by taking an overdose of barbiturates.
  • 1973 October, She begins a comeback tour with Giuseppe di Stefano. For the first time in eight years, Maria Callas is singing in public. It is clear from the first concert in Hamburg that the tour will be an artistic disaster.
  • 1975 March, Onassis dies - this is one of the major factors behind Maria's death.
  • 1977 September 16, Maria Callas passes away in her Paris home. Her funeral is held four days later.
